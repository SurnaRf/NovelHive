Jane Austen
Jane Austen (1775–1817) was an English novelist known for her insightful commentary on love, society, and class. Her famous works include Pride and Prejudice, Sense and Sensibility, and Emma. Austen's sharp wit and deep understanding of human relationships make her books timeless classics.
J.R.R. Tolkien
J.R.R. Tolkien (1892–1973) was an English writer, poet, and philologist, best known for creating the epic world of Middle-earth. His works, including The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings, have inspired generations and established modern fantasy as a literary genre.
Alexandre Dumas
Alexandre Dumas (1802–1870) was a French writer whose adventurous novels captivated readers worldwide. His notable works, including The Three Musketeers and The Count of Monte Cristo, are celebrated for their vivid storytelling and memorable characters.
